What makes us different

We teach programming through African cultural elements using the Culturally Situated Design Tools created by ethnomathematician and the University of Michigan Professor, Ron Eglash.

Some schools and ICT NGOs in Kenya and Uganda use Scratch, a block based programming tool, to teach students coding. Though the Culturally Situated Design Tools also use block-based programming, we teach elements in the African culture, like cornrow braids, creating a deeper bond among the students
